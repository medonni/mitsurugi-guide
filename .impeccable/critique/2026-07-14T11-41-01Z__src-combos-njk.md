## Anti-Patterns Verdict
**LLM assessment:** Does not read as AI-generated. Strong, specific identity (mono kickers, gold/violet on purple-black, diamond dot) that reads as a real fan project, not a wiki or SaaS template. The combo-line IA (numbered steps + WHY / WATCH OUT tags) is genuinely thoughtful.
**Deterministic scan:** 2 warnings on the built markup — `overused-font` (Space Grotesk, a committed brand font, intentional) and `broken-image` (the JS-populated lightbox `<img>`, false positive). No slop families detected.

## What's Working
1. **Combo-line IA teaches sequencing, doesn't just list cards** — numbered steps (incl. 1.5/2.5 chain sub-steps) with WHY and WATCH OUT tags. Directly serves the "teach, don't catalog" principle.
2. **Non-generic identity** — detector confirms near-zero slop; the page could not be mistaken for a TCG wiki or corporate template.
3. **Honest scaffolding** — explicit "being documented" placeholder for undocumented 2-card lines instead of faking content; START/END framing per line.

## Priority Issues
- **[P1] Mid-combo card names aren't links.** Step text names Saji, Kusanagi, Aramasa, etc. in bold but unlinked, so the newcomer half of the audience must recall each effect. Undercuts the page's core comprehension goal. **Fix:** link mid-combo card references to `/cards/#id` (data lives in `src/_data/combos.js`).
- **[P2] "2 Card Combos" is an empty dead-end.** The nav sub-link lands on a near-empty section ("check back soon"). **Fix:** make the empty state teach or redirect (link to the 1-card lines / Variants) rather than a bare placeholder.
- **[P2] No in-page wayfinding as the page grows.** Only section anchors exist; a reader scrolls a long list to find a specific line. **Fix:** a sticky in-page line index or jump list.
- **[P3] Lightbox ships an empty `<img>` in the DOM.** Detector false-positive functionally, but it is an empty tag; a `src` set only on open avoids the flag.

## Persona Red Flags
**Jordan (first-timer to the deck):** hits unlinked card names mid-combo and edge jargon ("chain block", "NSS") with no inline definition — has to leave the page to decode.
**Casey (mobile, arrives mid-research):** long open combos are a text wall one-handed; no quick jump to a specific line means lots of thumb-scrolling. Collapsible summaries are large, thumb-friendly (good).
**Alex (competitive grinder):** wants to jump straight to one line; collapse-all helps skimming, but there's no search or per-line deep link surfaced.

## Minor Observations
- "collapse all" hint has no visible "expand all" counterpart once collapsed.
- Space Grotesk flag is an accepted brand decision — no action.

## Questions to Consider
- What if each combo line had a stable deep link + a compact index, so a returning grinder lands on the exact line?
- Should the empty 2-card section teach the concept (what a 2-card line adds) instead of just saying "soon"?
- Does a first-timer ever have to leave this page to understand a step? If yes, the links should come to them.
